2.MY CITY MY DREAM

Description

A 'smart city' is an urban region that is highly advanced in terms of overall infrastructure, sustainable real estate, communications and market viability. It is a city where information technology is the principal infrastructure and the basis for providing essential services to residents. It is a city built from the principles of living within environment means. The ultimate goal of such cities is also to eliminate all carbon waste (zero-carbon city), to produce energy entirely through renewable resources, and to merge the city harmoniously with the natural environment. Smart cities also have the intentions of stimulating economic growth, reducing poverty, using higher population densities, and therefore obtaining higher efficiency, and improving health.
Students will have to present their unique ideas of creating such a Smart City.

Objective

The students will have to prepare a deft Power Point Presentation implementing their ideas that are practical and can help in creating a smart city of their dream into a reality.

Objective

Einstein once said that “We can’t solve problems by using the same kind of thinking we used when we created them.” Engineers have a key role to play in creating and maintaining sustainable communities across the planet and we have to rise to the challenges we face very quickly. Knowing what we know today, delivering what we deliver today and using current tools and processes invites disaster. As Engineers, we need to adapt our thinking, embrace advocacy and business planning, technology and computer sciences, work across wider domains and ensure that cities are truly able to meet the full needs of our future and we are successful in building smart cities for the betterment of all.

Rules

  • Minimum 2 and maximum 4 students in a group.
  • Power point Presentation on “Building a Smart City”.
  • Duration of presentation 7 minutes and following it will be a question answer round by the judges for 3 minutes for each group.
  • Every group would be given points on the basis of their presentation, performance in the question answer round and the Judge decision would be final and binding to all.

3. AUTOCROSS

Description

ASME chapter NIT Silchar presents a race of the models based on aerodynamics, where all vehicles will have same source of power defined the organizers and there will be constrains on uses of material to manufacture it, then they have to participate in the race. And then the winners will be announced by our judges on the basis of judging criteria.

Objective

To help the participants in analysis about the effects of aerodynamics in the motion of the models and to use the elastic energy to produce mechanical energy.

Motivation

Aerodynamics is a significant factor in any type of vehicle design, including automobiles. It is important in the prediction of forces and moments acting on sailing vessels. The aerodynamics of internal passages is important in heating/ventilation, gas piping, and in automotive engines where detailed flow patterns strongly affect the performance of the engine. So, one should know about aerodynamics and its effects in order to make the motion of a model efficient.

Judging and Scoring Criteria

  • Time taken by the model to cover the given distance.
  • Strength and design of the model.

Rules

  • 3 participants in every group.

  • There will be a prelims round in which the strength and design of the model will be examined by our judges and some questions will be asked to every team about the model and mechanism of the model.
    The qualified teas will be eligible for the second round.
